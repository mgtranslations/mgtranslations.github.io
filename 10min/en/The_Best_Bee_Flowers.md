This list includes plants proven by Dr. Gordon Frankie, University of California, and colleagues to be best at attracting bees[^1]. Native Oregon plants proven to attract native bees are based on research by Aaron Anderson, Oregon State University Garden Ecology Lab[^2]. Sources: *California Bees & Blooms* by Frankie et al., and blogs.oregonstate.edu/gardenecologylab/[^3].

## Organized by Type of Plant

### Tree/Shrub

| Common Name      | Scientific Name                            | Bee Food      | Bloom Time             | Notes                                                                                              |
| :--------------- | :----------------------------------------- | :------------ | :--------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Bluebeard        | *Caryopteris* species                     | nectar        | midsummer, early fall | Includes varieties with blue to purple flowers and green to golden foliage[^4].                   |
| California Lilac | *Ceanothus* 'Dark Star', 'Julia Phelps'   | pollen        | spring, early summer  | Medium to large shrubs with blue flowers[^4].                                                     |
| Chaste Tree      | *Vitex agnus-castus*                      | pollen & nectar | summer, early fall   | Different flower colors to choose from[^4].                                                       |
| Manzanita        | *Arctostaphylos densiflora* 'Sentinel'    | pollen & nectar | late winter, early spring | Includes *A. densiflora* 'Howard McMinn' & 'Sentinel'[^4].                                        |
| Rosemary         | *Rosemarinus officinalis*                 | mostly nectar | summer                | Many sizes, growth habits, flower colors, and flowering periods[^4].                              |
