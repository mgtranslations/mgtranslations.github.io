# The Best Bee Flowers --- Proven by Horticultural Research [cite: 1]

This list includes plants proven by Dr. Gordon Frankie, University of California, and colleagues to be best at attracting bees[cite: 1]. Native Oregon plants proven to attract native bees are based on research by Aaron Anderson, Oregon State University Garden Ecology Lab[cite: 2]. Sources: California Bees & Blooms by Frankie et al, blogs.oregonstate.edu/gardenecologylab/[cite: 3].

## Organized by Type of Plant

| :--------------- | :----------------------------------------- | :------------ | :--------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Bluebeard        | Caryopteris species                        | nectar        | midsummer, early fall | Caryopteris species include varieties with blue to purple flowers and green to golden foliage [cite: 4] |
| California Lilac | Ceanothus 'Dark Star', 'Julia Phelps'      | pollen        | spring, early summer | Medium to large shrubs with blue flowers [cite: 4]                                                 |
| Chaste Tree      | Vitex agnus-castus                         | pollen&nectar | summer, early fall   | Different flower colors to choose from [cite: 4]                                                   |
| Manzanita        | Arctostaphylos densiflora 'Sentinel'       | pollen&nectar | late winter, early spring | Arctostaphylos species especially A. densiflora 'Howard McMinn' & A. densiflora 'Sentinel' [cite: 4]   |
| Rosemary         | Rosemarinus officinalis                    | mostly nectar | summer                 | Many sizes, growth habits, flower colors, and flowering period [cite: 4]                           |
